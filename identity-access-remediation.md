## Overview
This case study documents the remediation of administrative access issues that prevented system configuration and maintenance tasks.

The focus was on restoring functionality while reducing risk associated with unmanaged privileged access.

---

## Risk Context
The environment exhibited several identity-related risks:
- Broken or unknown administrative credentials
- Overreliance on shared administrator accounts
- Lack of clear control over privileged access

These conditions increased the risk of misuse and hindered secure system management.

---

## Assessment Actions
- Evaluated current administrative access mechanisms
- Identified gaps in privileged account control
- Determined a secure path to regain administrative access

---

## Remediation
- Regained administrative access in a controlled manner
- Standardized local administrator configuration
- Implemented a managed administrative access model
- Ensured system management and monitoring tools were functional

---

## Outcome
- Restored administrative functionality
- Reduced risk of privilege misuse
- Improved system manageability and security posture

---

## Security Skills Demonstrated
- Identity and access management fundamentals
- Privileged access remediation
- Least privilege enforcement
- Secure system administration
