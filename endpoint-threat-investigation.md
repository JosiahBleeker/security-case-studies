## Overview
This case study covers the investigation and remediation of a user-reported malware incident on a production workstation.

The goal was to determine whether the activity represented an active threat, contain any risk, and restore the endpoint to a secure operational state.

---

## Risk Context
Reported symptoms suggested possible compromise, creating risks such as:
- Potential malware persistence
- User exposure to social engineering
- Business disruption from compromised endpoints

---

## Investigation Actions
- Triaged endpoint security alerts
- Reviewed system behavior and running processes
- Analyzed indicators associated with the reported activity
- Determined whether the threat was malicious or benign

---

## Findings
- Activity was identified as adware and scareware designed to coerce user interaction
- No evidence of persistence, privilege escalation, or lateral movement
- Endpoint protection tooling remained active and functional

---

## Remediation
- Removed all malicious and unwanted components
- Verified endpoint security agent health and protection status
- Ensured no residual artifacts remained
- Educated the user on phishing and scam indicators

---

## Outcome
- Endpoint returned to a clean operational state
- No further malicious activity observed
- Reduced likelihood of repeat user-driven incidents

---

## Security Skills Demonstrated
- Endpoint alert triage
- Threat classification and validation
- Incident response fundamentals
- User security awareness reinforcement
